  • 1849

    Harriet Tubman

    Harriet Tubman was an American abolitionist and political activist. Born into slavery, Tubman escaped and subsequently made 13 missions to rescue approximately 70 enslaved people, including family and friends, using the network of antislavery activists and safe houses known as the Underground Railroad.

  • 1945

    Anne Frank

    Anne Frank was a teenage Jewish girl who kept a diary while her family was in hiding from the Nazis during World War II. For two years, she and seven others lived in a "Secret Annex" in Amsterdam before being discovered and sent to concentration camps. Anne died in the Bergen-Belsen camp in 1945.Frank's father was the family's sole survivor. He decided to publish the diary, which gives a detailed account of Anne's thoughts, feelings and experiences while she was in hiding. It has been an international bestseller for decades and a key part of Holocaust education programs. Several humanitarian organizations are devoted to her legacy."Anne was a lively and talented girl, expressing her observations, feelings, self-reflections, fears, hopes and dreams in her diary," said Annemarie Bekker of the Anne Frank House in Amsterdam. "Her words resonate with people all around the world."

  • 1955

    Rosa Parks

    Rosa Parks was a civil rights activist.

    Parks was active in the fight for racial justice and became the secretary for the Montgomery chapter of the NAACP in 1943. However, Parks is more widely known for her bus journey home from work in 1955.

    Parks took a seat at the front of the bus, which was deemed the white section under segregation laws. The driver asked Parks to stand up and give her seat to a white man. Three Black individuals obeyed the driver’s order and moved to the back of the bus, but Parks refused. It was this journey home that jumpstarted the civil rights movement.

  • 1969

    Maya Angelou

    Maya Angelou was a poet, singer, memoirist, and civil rights activist. Angelou had a difficult childhood growing up in Arkansas. Not only did she experience racial prejudices and discrimination she was also traumatised as she faced abuse from her mother’s boyfriend. This led to Angelou becoming mute for years.

    Despite these early challenges, Angelou broke literacy history, as her award-winning memoir ‘I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings’ became the first non-fiction best-seller by an African-American woman. Angelou went on to become one of the greatest voices in the civil rights movement.

    Angelou shared her passion for literature with the world and how it can help those who were silenced find their voices once again.

  • 2012

    Malala Yousafzai

    Malala Yousafzai is an advocate for female education and the youngest nobel prize laureate. In early 2009 at just 11 years old she wrote anonlymously for BBC Urdu about her life under during the pakastani taliban occupation of swat. She rose to prominence in the news, and was nominated for a children's Peace prize by Desmond Tutu. On 9th October 2012 while on a bus after taking an exam, herself and two other females were shot by a Pakastani Taliban Gunman in retaliation to her activism. Malala was shot in the head. Following her recovery in the uk she co-funded the 'Malala fund' and became a education rights hero. She finished her high school education and went to oxford university.

  • 2014

    Emma Watson

    Emma Watson was named on of the 100 most influential people in the world by time magazine in 2015. As well as being a child star in the famous harry potter blockbusters, she is well known for her women's rights work. In 2014 she was appointed a UN goodwill Ambassador and helped to launch the UN campaign HeForShe, which advocates for gender equality. Watson was appointed to a G7 advisory body for womens' rights in 2019, and consulted with leaders on foreign policy. She has travelled to places like bangladesh and zambia promoting education for girls, ANd made her famous address at the UN Headquarters in New York City, where she questioned gender-based assumptions, and inspired many girls to be proud to call themselves a feminist.
